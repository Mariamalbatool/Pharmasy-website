A drug is defined according to US law as any substance (other than food or devices and tools) intended for use in the diagnosis, recovery, sedation, treatment, or prevention of disease, or intended to affect the structure or function of the body. This comprehensive definition of a drug, while important for legal purposes, is complex and not for everyday use. Therefore, there is a more simple - yet practical - definition of a drug, which is that it is any chemical or biological substance that affects the body and its processes.

Understanding which drug group a drug belongs to is useful, as drugs are usually grouped into therapeutic groups — that is, according to the disorder or symptom they are used to treat. Medications within each therapeutic group are categorized into classes or classes. Some classes depend on how the drugs work in the body to elicit their effects.
